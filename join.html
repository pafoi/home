Join and participate.

You can step into PaFoI as an individual, a small group, or as an organisation holding a challenge. You don’t need a finished idea — curiosity is enough.

Roles

How people join PaFoI.

Individuals

  • Explorer. Join dialogues and story circles to listen, learn, and connect.
  • Contributor. Take part in labs and sprints, sharing your experience and ideas.
  • Builder. Commit some time to help grow a project or theme that resonates with you.

Organisations

  • Challenge owner. Bring a question, issue, or opportunity to explore with others.
  • Ecosystem partner. Co‑host sessions, invite your communities, or hold a theme over time.
  • Implementation partner. Help steward and implement ideas that emerge from PaFoI.
Ways to join

Simple ways to start.

Many people begin with a single conversation or session. From there, we see together what feels alive and what next step makes sense.

  • Join an open session. Take part in a dialogue, lab, or sprint hosted with our partners.
  • Invite PaFoI into your context. Co‑design a small process around your community or theme.
  • Bring a project in progress. Use PaFoI as a space to reflect, reframe, and reconnect.

Support

Getting support for your project.

If you are working with a community or initiative and want a co‑creation space around it, PaFoI might be able to help. We start with a short conversation to understand context.

  • What is the question or issue you’re sitting with?
  • Who is already involved or affected?
  • What has already been tried, and what feels stuck?

From there, we can suggest a light‑touch process — sometimes one session, sometimes a short series — and explore who else might need to be in the room.

Principles

How we hold participation.

Joining PaFoI means joining a shared practice of care. A few simple commitments guide how we work together.

  • Care for time and energy. We try to be clear about what sessions are for and what they ask of people.
  • Respect for stories. Personal stories are treated with care; people decide what can travel and what stays in the room.
  • Clear expectations. We are honest about what PaFoI can and cannot offer at any given moment.
  • Learning together. We invite feedback and reflection after sessions so the forest can keep evolving.
