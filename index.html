Ideas are like seeds

We are often quick to judge or dismiss ideas—our own and others’. We call them “too idealistic”, “too ambitious”, or “not realistic enough”. Plant a Forest of Ideas (PaFoI) exists to create a different kind of space. A space where ideas are treated as seeds: small, fragile, but full of potential.

With time, care, and the right conditions, a seed can become a strong tree. The same is true for ideas. Not every seed will grow, and that’s okay. What matters is sowing, experimenting, and learning together until something takes root.

What we do

PaFoI helps people move from talking about change to trying things together.

Inspire

Talks, conversations, and storytelling sessions that spark curiosity and invite people to see their ideas and realities in new ways.

  • Public talks and dialogues
  • Story circles and reflection spaces

Co‑create

Co‑creation spaces where people explore challenges together and shape ideas into clearer concepts and opportunities.

  • Co‑creation labs and workshops
  • Foresight sprints and thematic explorations
  • Venture discovery tracks

Support

Gentle structures and connections to help promising ideas find the conditions they need to grow.

  • Clarifying and strengthening concepts
  • Connecting people and organisations
  • Exploring pathways into programmes, partnerships, or ventures

Who PaFoI is for

For individuals

PaFoI is for people who:

  • Have ideas but don’t yet know where to start
  • Want to contribute to themes they care about
  • Enjoy learning with others and building together

For organisations

PaFoI is for organisations that:

  • Work with communities and want to listen and co‑create
  • Have challenges they want to explore collaboratively
  • Support experimentation and ecosystem‑building

Themes and places we explore

PaFoI works through thematic “seasons” and place‑based collaborations. Sessions run online and in‑person, often co‑hosted with local partners.

Cities & communities Enterprise & livelihoods Food & agriculture Education & learning Health & wellbeing

Our initial focus is on Southeast Asia and neighbouring regions, while welcoming participation and ideas from elsewhere.

How we treat ideas and participation

Co‑creation only works when people feel safe to share.

  • You keep what you already have. Your existing work, tools, and intellectual property remain yours.
  • Shared session outputs. High‑level insights and frameworks from sessions may be shared (with attribution) so others can learn from them.
  • Growing ideas responsibly. When an idea looks like it could become a project or venture, we agree a time‑bound period to explore it together and decide how to move forward.
  • Respect and care. People and communities come before ideas. We pay attention to context, consent, and boundaries.

A fuller version of our participation principles and IP approach will be published soon.
