What we do inside PaFoI.

PaFoI is a set of simple, careful practices: listening with others, co‑creating ideas, and tending whatever looks like it might grow into something real.

Modes

Three ways we work with ideas.

Most collaborations move through some mix of these three modes. We keep them light, so they can be adapted to different people, places, and partners.

Inspire

Quiet talks, story circles, and gentle provocations that help people see their realities and possibilities differently.

  • Public conversations and fireside chats.
  • Story sharing from practitioners and communities.

Co‑create

Workshops and sprints where people map challenges, sketch ideas, and build shared language around what might be possible.

  • Co‑creation labs and thematic workshops.
  • Foresight and imagination sprints.
  • Venture discovery tracks.

Support

Gentle follow‑through for ideas that need more time: check‑ins, connections, and pathways into projects, programmes, or ventures.

  • Clarifying and framing ideas.
  • Introductions to potential collaborators.
  • Exploring possible “homes” for ideas.
Example journey

What a PaFoI journey can look like.

Each collaboration is different, but many follow a similar arc — from first questions to shared sketches to early‑stage projects.

  1. Starting from a question. A person or organisation brings a theme or challenge they care about.
  2. Listening together. We host one or more dialogues to surface perspectives, stories, and existing work.
  3. Co‑creating options. A lab or sprint turns insights into maps, ideas, and possible pathways.
  4. Tending what appears. A smaller group explores next steps, potential partners, and light structures for moving.
  5. Branching out. Some ideas grow into their own projects or hubs, sometimes with their own *.pafoi.org space.
Participation

How we handle ideas and contributions.

Co‑creation depends on trust. We keep our approach to participation and intellectual contributions simple and transparent.

  • You keep what you bring. Your existing work, tools, and IP remain yours.
  • Shared insights, careful details. High‑level patterns may be shared (with attribution); sensitive details stay in the circle.
  • Time‑bound exploration. If an idea looks like it could become a project or venture, we agree a short period to explore it together.
  • People before projects. We pay attention to capacity, consent, and context. No one is pushed beyond what feels right.
